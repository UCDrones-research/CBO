1.1 Bylaws

1.1.1 Purpose

  • Further aeronautical knowledge and skills through education and research
  • Provide legal and safe opportunities for members of the University of California to develop and utilize UAS

1.1.2 Membership

  • No discrimination

1.1.3 Management

  • Managed by the UC Center of Excellence on UAS Safety
  • UC UAS Advisory Board serves as well
  • Updates to safety guidelines made by UC UAS Advisory Board

1.1.4 Campus Management

  • A campus that wishes to have an active UC Drones Fixed-Flying Site management requires
    • Safety Lead
    • Faculty Lead
  • Safety Lead reports UAS activity to the UC Center of Excellence on UAS Safety
  • A Campus Designated Local Authority may concurrently act as the Safety Lead for the UC Drone FSP.
  • Campus is responsible for managing fixed-flying site activity and site coordination across the campus
  • Flying sites must be developed in collaboration with the facility manager and other space users
  • The Campus DLA and the UC Drones CBO

1.1.5 Grievance Procedure

The grievance procedure provides a mechanism to enforce existing safety rules by providing a progressive disciplinary system when needed. Most complaints can be resolved informally and should be rectified with training and education. If a complaint is serious or cannot be resolved informally, the matter should be referred to the UC UAS Advisory Board for its consideration by means of a Grievance Form to be filled out and turned into the Systemwide Designated UAS Authority. At least one witness is required to sign the Grievance Form.

The Systemwide Designated UAS Authority and the UC UAS Advisory Board shall use its judgment in carrying out action.

1.1.5.3 Retailation {#}

Any member receiving a Grievance, who directs any retaliation action against the person filing said grievance, will be subject to immediate expulsion from the program. This is to include threats, intimidation, physical harm, intentional equipment damage, or any other action deemed to be retaliatory.

1.2 Membership

Membership in the UC Drone Flying Site Program is free for all UC affiliates. Interested persons may sign up for the UC Drone FSP through the UC Drone Web App. Anyone already registered in UC Drones is automatically enrolled in the UC Drone FSP.

1.2.1 Requirements for Membership

  • Have a UC email account

  • Read and understand the UC Drone Flying Site safety guidelines

  • Completed the TRUST and has a TRUST certificate

1.2.2 Before your first flight

  • Complete the UC Drones orientation (link here:)
    • How to use UC Drones
    • Learn who your campus point of contact is
  • Review any campus Specific procedures or requirements

1.2.3 Types of Membership Benefits

  1. Student Pilot
  2. Research Pilot
  3. Flight Instructor
  • Allows use of UC Drone Fixed-Flying Sites and use of any educational or research special use guidelines such as flight operations over 400 ft or UAS over 55 lbs.
  • Automatic UAS liability coverage for UC buisness including research

1.2.3.1 Student Pilot

  • Student pilots have no expectation of experience
  • All flights must be coordinated by an Instructor or Academic Pilot
  • Non-Public areas allowed
  • Public areas with supervision

1.2.3.2 Academic Pilot

  • Have completed all drone training
  • May schedule coursework, research and practice flight activity

1.2.3.3 Flight Instructor

  • Have completed all drone training ()
  • Have completed instructor training (available online)
  • May schedule and coordinate drone flight activity
